Daspletosaurus

The robust tyrannosaurid of the Cretaceous

Daspletosaurus was a large carnivorous dinosaur that lived in North America about 77–74 million years ago, during the Cretaceous period. It was a close relative of Tyrannosaurus rex, but older and with a more compact and muscular body.

Physical Characteristics

  • Length: 8–9 meters
  • Weight: 2–3 tons
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Locomotion: Bipedal

Discovery and Fossils

The first Daspletosaurus fossil was discovered in 1921 in Canada, but it was only named in 1970. The discovered specimens show that it had a massive skull and more robust bones than other tyrannosaurids such as Albertosaurus.

Behavior and Ecology

This predator likely hunted large ceratopsids like Centrosaurus. Its short but muscular arms suggest they may have been used to hold prey during the attack. Bite marks on fossils indicate conflicts between individuals of the same species.

Scientific Classification

Quick Facts

Period: Late Cretaceous

Location: North America

Discovered: 1921 (named in 1970)

Name meaning: "Frightful lizard"

Fun Facts

  • Had denser bones than other tyrannosaurids
  • Had around 60 extremely sharp teeth
  • Bite marks on fossils suggest cannibalism
  • Possibly hunted using ambush tactics
